### Problem: Digital Monopolies

As the "Why Ocelot-Cloud?" article explains, Ocelot-Cloud removes the technical barriers to self-hosted infrastructure. But its social vision goes far beyond that. This <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fuFlMtZmvY0" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">short video</a> shows how profit-driven monopolies now control much of the power of the Internet, and what can be done about it.

### Solution: Digital Villages

A Digital Village is a small digital community of people who share a common interest, such as a neighborhood, friends, a hobby, a cause, or a profession. Because of its small size, its IT infrastructure is not demanding and can be hosted on a single server that provides all the software the community needs, such as forums, data clouds, etc. The Ocelot-Cloud makes it easy to create and maintain such a digital village. Hardware costs are minimal and financed by the community. Like a real village, it is exclusive and members know each other well. This decentralization has many advantages over digital monopolies:

* **No commercial incentives** to spy on users, encourage addictive behavior, or push destructive content
* **No single point of control**, reducing censorship and allowing diverse, self-regulated communities
* **Freedom of self-organization**, where each village sets its own culture and rules, perhaps even using democratic voting systems
* **Optional reputation-based systems**, like a real village, discouraging harmful behavior to other members through social accountability or even expulsion from the village
* **Data sovereignty**, because data remains on locally controlled servers, rather than externally where it can be stolen without notice
* **No absolute power by any entity**; while villages need a trusted admin, they can easily create a copy village and move there if that trust is broken

This positive vision of a better world seeks to improve the quality of Internet life and return power to the people. The goal is not to decentralize the entire Internet, but it is worth considering decentralizing key aspects of social Internet life.
